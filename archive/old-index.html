The Upskilling Labs

Welcome to The Upskilling Labs

A new way of working — not just AI

Upskilling isn't only about learning AI tools. We believe AI creates a new way of working: we become directors and managers of the work, not just the ones doing it. That shift puts a premium on what you already bring. Your experience and judgment are the inputs that make AI useful — if you don't understand a problem deeply, you'll get bad outputs.

AI has made answers cheap. It hasn't made judgment cheap.

What we offer

Take charge of your future of work

Here you take charge of your own future of work. You join a community to learn alongside, not a course. You take on real problems, showcase what you make, and build a portfolio along the way — from research to prototype. And you're supported the whole time: volunteer mentors who step in when you're stuck, hands-on workshops, open office hours, and a growing learning library.

Where we started

Where we started

The Upskilling Labs was born in Washington, DC — out of a pilot that DC Public Library and Levy Strategic Design built in fall 2025. In January 2026, we became our own community.

Where we're going

We're just getting started

Right now, the Labs is entirely volunteer-powered — people here because they believe in the work. But we have big ambitions. What began locally is growing into something national, so more people can learn by doing wherever they are. We're figuring out this uncertain future together.

Our definition

What "upskilling" means at the Labs

We're not a course platform. Upskilling here means learning through real, collaborative work. Upskillers take on genuine problems, form small teams (pods), and ship something — from research to prototype — over a multi-week cycle.

As a mentor, you're supporting that process, not teaching a curriculum. Your real-world judgment is the asset. Upskillers bring the problems; you help them think better.
